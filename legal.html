Affiliate disclosure

Myse is free. Some links to grocery stores and prep gear are affiliate links: if you buy through one, Myse may earn a small commission, at no extra cost to you. It never changes your price, and it never changes which recipes or products appear — Myse only links the stores and gear a meal-prep kitchen genuinely uses.

As an Amazon Associate, I earn from qualifying purchases.

Myse also participates in the Walmart affiliate program (through the Impact network) and the Instacart Developer Platform affiliate program. When you tap a store button, those companies may set a cookie to credit a resulting purchase to Myse.

What Myse stores about you

Signed out, everything you do stays in your own browser, in local storage on your device. It is not sent to Myse, and clearing your browser's site data removes it.

Signed in, the things that belong to your account are also stored in Myse's database (hosted by Supabase), so they can follow you across devices and survive a cleared browser:

  • your favorites and standing allergies;
  • your nutrition profile (the height, weight, age, sex, and goal used to estimate a calorie target) and your log of weight, lifts, and runs;
  • your ratings and reviews;
  • any recipes you post to the Community board, and their photos;
  • any threads and replies you post to the forum.

Ratings, reviews, Community recipes, and forum posts are public: the star average is shown to every visitor, and a review, a recipe, or a forum post you write appears under the display name on your account, along with any photo you add. Everything else in the list is private to you — the database only ever answers for the signed-in account that owns the rows.

Two things stay in the browser only, even signed in: your weekly plan (and its shopping list) and whether Myse Plus is active on this device.

Clearing your browser does not remove what's stored with your account — deleting the account does (see “Your choices”).

When data does leave your device

  • Signing in. Accounts are handled by Supabase, which processes your email address (and, for Google or Apple sign-in, the name and email those providers share) to sign you in, and hosts the account data listed above. If you never sign in, none of this applies.
  • Building an Instacart cart. If you use “Build my Instacart cart,” the ingredient names from your shopping list are sent to Instacart (through a small proxy service) so it can create the cart. Nothing else about you is sent.
  • Tapping a store or gear link. That takes you to Amazon, Walmart, or Instacart, where their own privacy policies apply.
  • Fonts. Pages load typefaces from Google Fonts, so Google may receive your device's IP address as part of serving them.

Myse does not serve third-party advertising networks, and it does not sell your personal information.

Community recipes

Signed in, you can post your own recipes to the Community board. Anything you post is public: the recipe, its photo, and your account's display name are shown to every visitor, and other people can review, favorite, and plan it. Its nutrition and allergen information is self-declared by whoever posted it, and is not verified by Myse — treat community recipes accordingly, and if an allergy is severe, check every ingredient yourself.

Only post recipes and photos you have the right to share, and nothing unlawful, offensive, or infringing someone else's copyright. You can delete a recipe you posted at any time from your profile, which removes it and its photo for everyone. Any signed-in visitor can report a recipe, and one that draws enough reports is automatically hidden from the board pending review; Myse may also remove a recipe that breaks these rules. Photos are stored in Supabase Storage and are resized in your browser before upload, which also strips location and other embedded metadata.

Community forum

The forum is public. Threads and replies you post are shown to every visitor under your account's display name, and reading them needs no account. The same rules apply as for community recipes: don't post anything unlawful, offensive, or infringing; you can delete your own posts at any time; any signed-in visitor can report a thread or reply, and one that draws enough reports is automatically hidden pending review; and Myse may remove anything that breaks these rules. The forum is for support and discussion, not medical advice — be kind, and see a professional for guidance on your health.

Myse Plus

Myse Plus is an optional paid tier ($2.99/month with a 14-day free trial, or $29.99 once) that unlocks printing, PDF export, the weekly plan view, and the calorie target, and removes sponsored tickets. In the mobile apps, billing is handled by the app store you subscribed through — cancellation and refunds follow that store's rules, and cancelling stops future charges without deleting your account or anything you've saved. In the current demo build no real payment is taken.

Not medical advice

The calorie target is an estimate from a population equation, and the log is a notebook, not a coach. Nothing on Myse is medical advice, and none of it replaces a clinician or a registered dietitian — especially around weight loss. Recipe and allergen data are illustrative: if an allergy is severe, always verify every label yourself.

Children

Myse isn't directed to children, and its calorie-target tool deliberately refuses to compute a target for anyone under 18.

Your choices

You can use the whole site without an account, skip the store links entirely, and clear your browser-only data at any time through your browser's settings. Signed in, Delete my account on the profile page permanently removes the account and every server-side row that belongs to it — favorites, allergies, nutrition profile, log, ratings, reviews, any Community recipes you posted (and their photos), and your forum threads and replies. Blocking cookies or third-party requests will still leave the recipes fully usable.

Changes & contact

If this policy changes, the date at the top will change with it. Questions about privacy or these disclosures can go to the project's public repository at github.com/Big-Sweat/meal-prep.
